SAT Redesign

Q4 2024 - Gobierno de México

Challenge:

Between 2012 and 2024, little progress was made in making processes for complying with tax obligations in Mexico more digital. As part of the presidential initiative to simplify official procedures, it was proposed that the SAT's (Servicio de Administración Tributaria) online site be reviewed and redesigned to make it easier for new taxpayers to register.

Outcome:

A new landing page aimed at streamlining registration for new users and help them find the most appropriate regime according to their economic activities, while avoiding causing additional friction for users who are already familiar with the current portal.

Time frame: 1 month

Research:

Initially, a diagnosis of the available site was performed. Following work meetings with personnel from the Secretaría de Hacienda, it was identified that new taxpayers were experiencing difficulties with registration and understanding the benefits and obligations of the available tax regimes.

Additionally, the fragmented nature of the information across many microsites made navigation complex, with duplicated and sometimes outdated or contradictory information. As part of an internal SAT initiative, the number of entries was cut from around 800 to 200 by eliminating duplicate and outdated records.

Insights:

Due to time constraints, the process did not include research conducted directly with users of the platform. Instead, it started from the analysis conducted by the Tax Administration Service's design and communication team, orienting the design towards two potential user profiles.

Identified issues:

  • Problematic information hierarchy.
  • Unintuitive Iconography
  • The layout of the elements on the screen was prone to overflowing.
  • Lack of accessibility for users with disabilities
  • Lack of visual unity with other Mexican government portals.

Design solution:

Given the limited time for the generation of the solution, a medium fidelity prototype was proposed, incorporating an interactive and granular questionnaire that would allow users to identify a regime suitable for their economic activities
